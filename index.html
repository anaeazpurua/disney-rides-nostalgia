How nostalgic is Disney's Magic Kingdom? A very personal analysis


By Ana Elena Azp√∫rua

Nostalgia is one of Walt Disney World's magic ingredients. Going back to the same attraction you experience as a child can transport you to a time of happiness, or at least the fantasy of it. During a recent visit with my kids, to what extent was I reliving the past? How many of the rides we took were a new experience for me? What's the ratio of old to new memories at "the happiest place on Earth."


Magic Kingdom has 38 attractions operating today. A little more than half (55%) were open when my parents took me to the famous theme park in 1993. Assuming I rode all those rides and enjoyed all the attractions back then, means that my visit in June 2023 had a 50/50 mix of nostalgic attractions --those that I ejoyed 20 years ago-- and new experiencies.

How did this combination of old and new attractions has changed from one generation to the next? How was it for my parents when they brought me in 1993? It seems their trip was mainly nostalgic, if measured by the attractions that remained since their previous visit in 1975. Only two were new experiences for them --Big Thunder Mountain and Splash Mountain (now closed). In other words, for every new memory, there were ten nostalgic rides.

Click on each star to see the name of the attraction.



About half of the attractions offered at the theme park in 2023 are new compared to 1993, but that doesn't mean they just opened... On average, the attractions at Magic Kingdom are 34 years, but half of them have been around for 48 or more years.

Nostalgic rides are a big component of Magic Kigdom. Adventureland, the land of "Pirates of the Caribbean" and "Jungle Cruise", hasn't had a new ride opening since "The Magic Carpets of Aladdin", more than 20 years ago. Frontierland hasn't had a big debut since "Splash Mountain" in 1992. Disney plans to launch "Tiana's Bayou's Adventure" in 2024 to replace it after it closed it due to allegations of racism.
