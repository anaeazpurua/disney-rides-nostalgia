Banking on Nostalgia? A Very Personal Analysis of Disney's Magic Kingdom


By Ana Elena Azpúrua

Nostalgia is one of Walt Disney World's magic ingredients. Going back to the same attraction you experience as a child can transport you to a time of happiness, or at least the fantasy of it. During a recent visit with my kids, how much was I reliving the past? How many of the rides I took were new? What's the combination of old and new memories at "the happiest place on Eath."


The Magic Kingdom has 38 attractions operating today. A little more than half (55%) were open when my parents took me to the famous theme park in 1993. Assuming I rode all those rides and enjoyed all the attractions back then, means that my visit in June 2023 had a 50/50 mix of nostalgic attractions --those that I ejoyed 20 years ago-- and new experiencies.

I wondered how this combination of old and new attractions had changed over time. How was it for my parents when they brought me in 1993? It seems their trip was mainly nostalgic, if measured by the attractions that remained since their previous visit in 1975. Only two were new experiences for them --Big Thunder Mountain and Splash Mountain (now closed).
